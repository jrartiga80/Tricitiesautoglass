Common Questions

Auto Glass FAQ

Does my insurance cover windshield replacement?
Many comprehensive auto insurance policies cover glass repair and replacement with no out-of-pocket cost to you. Washington state does not waive the deductible on glass claims, but many policies have a separate, lower glass deductible. Call us and we'll help you check your coverage before we start any work.
Can a cracked windshield actually be repaired, or does it need to be replaced?
Chips smaller than a quarter and cracks shorter than about 6 inches can usually be repaired with resin injection. Cracks larger than that, or anything in the driver's direct line of sight, typically require a full replacement. We'll give you an honest assessment when we look at it — no pressure to upgrade if a repair is the right call.
How long does a windshield replacement take?
Most windshield replacements take 60–90 minutes from start to finish. After installation, we recommend leaving the car parked for at least an hour to let the adhesive cure fully before driving. We'll let you know the exact safe-drive-away time based on conditions that day.
Do you come to my home or do I drive to you?
We come to you. Our mobile units are fully equipped to handle repairs and replacements at your home, workplace, or any parking lot in the Tri-Cities area. There's no shop to drive to — that's kind of the whole point.
What areas do you serve?
We primarily serve Kennewick, Richland, and Pasco, plus surrounding communities including West Richland, Benton City, Prosser, Finley, Burbank, Connell, and Hermiston, OR. If you're not sure whether we cover your location, just call and we'll tell you right away.
Is the glass you use as good as the original?
Yes. We use OEM-equivalent glass that meets the same safety and fit specifications as the glass that came from the factory. For newer vehicles with ADAS cameras and sensors in the windshield, we also handle proper recalibration so your safety systems work correctly after replacement.
